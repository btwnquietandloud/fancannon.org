EXPANDING THE UNIVERSE

The Expanse didn't end when the TV show wrapped or the last book was published. Through novellas, comics, games, and an upcoming AAA video game, the universe keeps growing — and its influence on sci-fi culture runs deeper than ever.

📚 THE NOVELLAS

📖

Nine Stories That Fill the Gaps

2011–2022 · Compiled in Memory's Legion (2022)

Between the nine mainline novels, Daniel Abraham and Ty Franck wrote nine novellas that fill crucial gaps in the story. These aren't optional extras — they contain character origins, key plot bridges, and moments that recontextualize the entire series. In 2022, all nine were compiled into Memory's Legion, which also includes a brand new story.

"The Butcher of Anderson Station"

2011

Fred Johnson's backstory — the massacre that turned a UN colonel into the Belt's most important ally.

"Gods of Risk"

2012

Bobbie Draper's nephew David gets caught up in drug manufacturing on Mars. A quiet, human story.

"Drive"

2012

Solomon Epstein's fatal test flight — the accidental invention that made interplanetary civilization possible.

"The Churn"

2014

Amos Burton's origin story in Baltimore. Meet "Timmy" and the crime boss Lydia who shaped him. Essential reading.

"The Vital Abyss"

2015

Told from inside a prison, a Protogen scientist reflects on the Eros experiment. Chilling and clinical.

"Strange Dogs"

2017

Children on Laconia discover alien repair drones that can resurrect the dead. Foreshadows everything Duarte does.

"Auberon"

2019

A Laconian governor arrives on a colony world run by a crime boss. Old-Earth corruption patterns repeat on new worlds.

"The Sins of Our Fathers"

2022

Filip Nagata's life on the colony world Jannah after the gates close. The series' emotional epilogue.

"The Last Flight of the Cassandra"

2019

RPG-exclusive short story included in The Expanse Roleplaying Game. Canon, per the authors.

🎨 THE COMICS

💥

The Expanse: Dragon Tooth

BOOM! Studios · 12 Issues · 2023–2024 · Written by Andy Diggle

The most ambitious Expanse expansion outside the novels. Dragon Tooth is a 12-issue comic series that bridges the 30-year gap between the TV show's finale (Babylon's Ashes) and the unadapted Book 7 (Persepolis Rising). It fills in what happened to the Rocinante crew, the Transport Union, and the rising threat of Laconia during those missing decades.

The Kickstarter campaign raised $1.4 million — smashing BOOM! Studios' record. The series was overseen by Ty Franck and Daniel Abraham, making it essentially canon. Collected in three trade paperback volumes.

BOOM! Studios · 2025 · Co-written by Wes Chatham (Amos Burton)

The Expanse: Origins

ComiXology · 4 Issues · 2017

Four digital comics released alongside the TV show's early seasons, each exploring the origin story of a core Rocinante crew member: Holden, Naomi, Alex, and Amos. Short but illuminating character studies.

🎲 THE TABLETOP RPG

🎯

The Expanse Roleplaying Game

Green Ronin Publishing · 2019–Present · AGE System

It's fitting that The Expanse started as a tabletop RPG setting (originally called "2350") and has come full circle with an official, author-approved tabletop game. Published by Green Ronin, the game uses the Adventure Game Engine (AGE) rules and lets players create their own crews — Earthers, Martians, or Belters — navigating the same political powder keg as the novels.

The Kickstarter hit $194,000 (goal was $30,000) in under 24 hours. The game is considered canon by Abraham and Franck, and even includes an exclusive short story, "The Last Flight of the Cassandra."

The product line has grown to include four hardbound rulebooks, a GM kit, custom dice sets, a ships sourcebook with detailed deckplans, and a series of "Trades of The Expanse" PDFs covering professions from bounty hunters to journalists. A Transport Union Edition was Kickstarted in 2024 to update the game with content through the full series.

♟️ THE BOARD GAME

🗺️

The Expanse Board Game

WizKids · 2017 · Designed by Geoff Engelstein

A political strategy board game designed by Geoff Engelstein in collaboration with the series authors. Players represent four factions — Earth's UN, Mars's MCRN, the OPA, and Protogen Inc. — competing for dominance of the solar system through fleets, influence, and event cards featuring characters and moments from the series.

The game draws inspiration from Twilight Struggle but plays in about 60 minutes. Each card represents a character or event from the books, creating a tense game of political maneuvering that captures the series' themes of power, conspiracy, and fragile alliances.

🎮 THE VIDEO GAMES

🔥

The Expanse: Osiris Reborn

Owlcat Games · Coming 2026 · PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC · Action RPG

The most anticipated Expanse media in years. Osiris Reborn is a third-person action RPG developed by Owlcat Games (the studio behind Pathfinder and Warhammer 40K: Rogue Trader). Announced at the Future Games Show in June 2025, it immediately drew comparisons to Mass Effect — and the developers embrace the comparison.

You play as a Pinkwater Security mercenary on shore leave at Eros Station when everything goes sideways. Create your own captain — Earther, Martian, or Belter — and take command of the solar system's most advanced ship. The game spans from the events of Leviathan Wakes through Caliban's War, meaning protomolecule horrors, Eros, and the Ganymede Incident are all on the table.

Features include:

• Custom character creation with three origin backgrounds affecting dialogue and relationships
• Companion system with deep relationships, personal stories, and romance options
• Third-person cover-based combat with squad commands and real-time tactics
• Consequences that matter — your choices shape relationships and outcomes across the solar system
• TV show actors reprising roles from the original series

Nominated for Best Game Trailer and Most Wanted Game at the Golden Joystick Awards 2025. No exact release date yet, but 2026 is confirmed for PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC (Steam, Epic, GOG).

"It's a very good match for us because of the stories we want to tell."
— Owlcat Games, on choosing The Expanse

🕹️

The Expanse: A Telltale Series

Telltale Games · 2023 · 5 Episodes · PC, PS4/5, Xbox

A narrative-driven adventure game from the revived Telltale Games, set before the events of the TV show. Players control Camina Drummer — the Belter leader who became a fan favorite — in her earlier years, making choices that shape her path and the people around her.

The game captures the intimate, character-driven storytelling that made the show special, with branching dialogue and moral dilemmas that feel authentically Expanse. It proved there was a massive appetite for Expanse games, directly paving the way for Osiris Reborn.

🌍 THE CULTURAL ZEITGEIST

✨

Why The Expanse Still Matters

2015–Present · And counting

The Expanse isn't just a good sci-fi series — it fundamentally changed what audiences expect from space opera. Before The Expanse, "realistic" sci-fi on TV meant sterile corridors and technobabble. After it, audiences demand physics that work, politics that matter, and characters who feel like real people navigating impossible systems.

The show's rescue from cancellation by Amazon — driven by a massive fan campaign that included a plane towing a #SaveTheExpanse banner and bezos personally tweeting the announcement — became one of the most famous examples of fan activism saving a show. It proved that passionate niche audiences could move mountains.

The series' themes have become eerily prescient: income inequality between inner planets and Belt mirrors real-world wealth gaps; pandemic lockdowns echoed the Eros quarantine; the protomolecule's weaponization parallels debates about dangerous technologies; and Marco's radicalization arc reads like a case study in extremism. Critics have called it "the most politically relevant sci-fi of the decade."

The authors, Abraham and Franck, continue to shape sci-fi — their new book series has already been optioned for adaptation by the same team. The show's influence echoes through every new space series that tries to get the physics right, the politics messy, and the characters human.
