Songs of Steele's

ORIGINAL MUSIC FROM THE BELINDA BLINKED UNIVERSE

Three original compositions inspired by Rocky Flintstone's six-book saga of pots, pans, and passion. From anthems to power ballads to Europop, the Belinda Blinked universe has a soundtrack it never asked for and absolutely deserves.

Why Belinda Blinked Needs Music

Every great literary universe has a soundtrack. Lord of the Rings has Howard Shore. Star Wars has John Williams. Harry Potter has hedwig's theme. Belinda Blinked has... well, until now it had nothing. Which is remarkable for a series where Des Martin plays the ocarina at a corporate farewell party.

These songs fill that void. They're original fan compositions that capture the spirit of Rocky's masterwork — the absurdity, the sincerity, the black thongs, the geography crimes, and the eternal question: what happened to Bill?

Each song takes a different musical approach, because the Belinda Blinked universe contains multitudes. It's simultaneously a corporate drama, a spy thriller, an erotic novel, and a family comedy podcast. No single genre could contain it. So we used three.

🎵 Where Did Bill Go?
0:000:00

The most emotionally devastating song ever written about a fictional HR director who appeared in one chapter and was never mentioned again. Sung from Bill's perspective as a full-throated 80s power ballad — soft piano building to a massive reverb-drenched crescendo — the comedy comes entirely from the contrast between the earnest, heartbroken delivery and the absurdity of what he's actually upset about.

The bridge is the centerpiece: the hosts asked Rocky about Bill in the final episode. Rocky said "Who?" The song treats this single word as the most devastating moment in literary history. There is a key change. There are tears. Bill is still missing.

🎵 Black Thong (The Steele's Anthem)
0:000:00

The absolute stupidest song on this page and therefore the most accurate representation of the source material. A full-throttle 90s Europop dance track — think Vengaboys meets Aqua — built around the single most consistent detail in all six Belinda Blinked books: every character wears a black thong. Every. Single. One.

Men, women, aristocrats, spies, regional sales managers — Rocky never specifies any other undergarment across the entire series. This song celebrates that commitment to a singular sartorial vision. The spoken bridge where the singer realizes the scale of Rocky's thong obsession is the moment the song transcends parody and becomes something genuinely philosophical.

Warning: This song will be stuck in your head for approximately six to eight business days. Steele's Pots and Pans accepts no liability.
